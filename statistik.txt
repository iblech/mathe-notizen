=== James--Stein estimator

The James–Stein estimator is a biased estimator of the mean of Gaussian random
vectors. It can be shown that the James–Stein estimator dominates the
"ordinary" least squares approach, i.e., it has lower mean squared error on
average. It is the best-known example of Stein's phenomenon.

=== Relevanz der Stichprobengröße bei Geschlechtsunterschiedsdebatten

Merim Bilalic, Kieran Smallbone, Peter McLeod, Fernand Gobet.
Why are (the best) women so good at chess? Participation rates and
gender differences in intellectual domains.
https://rspb.royalsocietypublishing.org/content/royprsb/276/1659/1161.full.pdf

A popular explanation for the small number of women at the top level of
intellectually demanding activities from chess to science appeals to
biological differences in the intellectual abilities of men and women.

An alternative explanation is that the extreme values in a large sample
are likely to be greater than those in a small one. Although the
performance of the 100 best German male chess players is better than
that of the 100 best German women, we show that 96 per cent of the
observed difference would be expected given the much greater number of
men who play chess.

There is little left for biological or cultural explanations to account
for. In science, where there are many more male than female
participants, this statistical sampling explanation, rather than
differences in intellectual ability, may also be the main reason why
women are under-represented at the top end.
